I can babble on about my academic achievements and my zeal for self-improvement in all aspects of my life, but what better way to demonstrate the point than with an example.

The implications of lockdown loomed over us all in different ways. For me, it meant that I had stayed locked in doors for months on end. Granted, it did bless me with the time to self-reflect and realise the newly found hobby of programming which I now plan to pursue as a career, it wasn’t all good news. Let’s just say being locked up at home for too long does not do wonders for your physical or mental well-being.

In the spirit of self-improvement, a few months ago I had decided to challenge myself to go out for a 10km jog 6am every morning for the rest of this year. Some mornings were dark, many were cold, some even rainy, and even with the regular feeling of soreness, I have successfully gone out every morning without fail and will continue to do so until the end of the year.

Quite recently, I have also challenged myself to become a full stack web developer in the space of a few months. I have no intention of quitting!
